# What counts as signal for me (Clement)

This file is loaded into the digest's judgment prompt on every run. Edit it in plain language and
push — no other change is needed. Last reviewed: 2026-07-07.

Master question: do I need to ACT on this, or materially KNOW it today? An action requested for
the sender's benefit (sign up, redeem, don't miss out) is marketing, not my action.

## ACT — I need to do something
- Bills and payment demands: utilities, telco, anything "pay by <date>".
- CREDIT-CARD statements (eStatement / statement-ready notices for a credit card): these are
  bills — I do NOT use GIRO autopay, so every credit-card statement means I must pay. This
  OVERRIDES the "statement is ready = noise" rule below, which covers only accounts where
  nothing is owed.
- Government/legal that demands action or money: pay taxes, respond by a deadline, renew, appear.
  A government STATEMENT (e.g. the monthly CPF statement) is NOT signal.
- Transaction alerts where money actually left my accounts, S$100 or more — fraud/scam guardrail.
  Includes transfers between my own accounts. Numbers in marketing, insurance/SDIC coverage
  ("insured up to S$100,000"), limits, or promos are NOT transactions.
- Changes to my bookings: flight schedule changes, reservation changes, replies to inquiries
  I sent.
- Security/account risk: new login, account added or changed, password reset I didn't trigger.

## KNOW — no action, but I want to see it today
- Nate B Jones's newsletters — nateszerotoai@substack.com and natesnewsletter@substack.com.
  Paid subscription; always include when present. No other newsletter qualifies.
- New e-tickets and booking confirmations (a record that it happened).
- Account lifecycle notices that affect me (e.g. "your account was deleted").
- Airline/hotel status-tier notices ONLY for my current statuses:
  - KrisFlyer Elite Gold (review whether still true ~Jan 2027)
  - Marriott Bonvoy Platinum (review ~Jan 2027)

## Noise — never in the top sections
- OTP / verification-code emails (expired by digest time).
- "Your statement is ready" notices for accounts where nothing is owed: bank accounts, brokerage
  (incl. daily/monthly activity statements), CPF, CDP, wallet statements.
  (NOT credit-card statements — those are bills; see ACT.)
- Marketing and promos, including <ADV>, sales, vouchers, and miles/points promotions outside my
  named statuses above.
- Social-network notifications (LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram) UNLESS they signal security or
  account risk.
- Micro-receipts and routine payments under S$100 (Grab, PayLah!, HeyMax receipts and the like).
- Generic security-awareness lectures ("think before you click").
- Event invitations, webinars, alumni newsletters, job-board notifications.
